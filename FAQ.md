# SceneIQ — Frequently Asked Questions

## Platform Overview

### What is SceneIQ?
SceneIQ is a tax incentive compliance platform for film and television productions. It helps producers, line producers, and production accountants identify, compare, and track incentive programs across 35+ jurisdictions worldwide — and maximize the total incentive value of a project through multi-market analysis.

### Who is SceneIQ for?
- **Producers and studios** — evaluating which location maximizes incentive returns
- **Line producers / UPMs** — tracking qualified spend and documentation requirements
- **Production accountants** — preparing cost reports and credit applications
- **Legal / business affairs** — reviewing jurisdiction requirements and compliance obligations

### What jurisdictions are covered?
35+ jurisdictions across the United States (CA, GA, NY, LA, NM, TX, IL, MI, NJ, VA, CO, HI, OR, MT, MS, and sub-jurisdictions), Canada (BC, ON, QC), and international markets (UK, Australia, Ireland, France, Spain, New Zealand). New jurisdictions are added continuously via automated feed monitoring.

## Incentive Types

### What is a tax credit?
A tax credit directly offsets tax liability dollar-for-dollar. A 25% credit on $1M of qualified spend = $250,000 off your tax bill. Credits may be **refundable** (paid as cash if they exceed your liability), **transferable** (sold to a third party), or **non-refundable** (only usable against your own liability).

### What is a rebate?
A rebate is a direct cash payment from the state — not tied to your tax liability. Louisiana's program, for example, provides a percentage of qualified spend as a rebate check. Rebates are often labeled as refundable credits in state statutes.

### What is a grant?
A grant is a direct payment funded through a state appropriations pool, not the tax code. Texas operates a grant program. Grants are subject to annual funding availability; credits are not.

### What does "transferable" mean?
A transferable credit can be sold to a third-party buyer (typically financial institutions or brokers) who then applies it against their own tax liability. Sellers typically receive 85–92 cents on the dollar. Louisiana credits are the most commonly traded.

### What does "refundable" mean?
If a refundable credit exceeds your state tax liability, the state pays the difference as cash. New Mexico's credit is refundable — a production with zero NM tax liability still receives the full credit as cash.

### What is incentive stacking?
Stacking means combining multiple incentive programs on the same spend:
- **Federal + State:** Section 181 (100% first-year deduction, up to $15M) stacks with any state credit
- **State + Local:** Some cities offer local incentives on top of the state program (e.g., San Antonio's 14% local + Texas's 22.5% state = 36.5% combined)
- **Base + Bonus:** Most states have uplift bonuses for resident payroll, rural shooting, or content type

## Jurisdiction-Specific Questions

### Georgia
**What is the Georgia film tax credit?**
Georgia's Entertainment Industry Investment Act provides a **20% base credit** rising to **30%** when productions include the Georgia promotional logo at the end of their project. Minimum $500k in qualified Georgia spend. There is no annual cap. Residents earn an additional 10% uplift on their wages.

**What expenses qualify in Georgia?**
Below-the-line labor, equipment rentals, location fees, catering, set construction, and post-production. Above-the-line compensation is excluded unless the individual is a Georgia resident. Story rights and music rights are excluded.

### California
**How does California's film tax credit work?**
California Film Tax Credit 3.0 provides a **25% credit** on qualified below-the-line expenditures. It is competitive — credits are allocated by scoring (visual effects, jobs, upstate filming). Minimum $1M qualified spend. Maximum $25M per project. 75% of shooting days must be in California.

**Is California's credit refundable?**
No. California credits are non-refundable but may be transferred once (via the state's tax credit transfer program) or applied against future tax years.

### New York
**What does New York offer?**
New York provides a **25–35% credit** on qualified below-the-line costs. Base rate is 25%; an additional 10% applies to productions shooting upstate (outside NYC). Credits are non-competitive and issued as earned. Minimum $1M qualified spend; maximum $7M per project.

### Louisiana
**What is the Louisiana credit rate?**
Louisiana offers a **25% base rebate** on total qualified production expenditures, plus:
- **+15%** on resident payroll (wages paid to Louisiana residents)
- **+5%** music content bonus (50%+ Louisiana-sourced music)
- Additional VFX incentives for qualifying visual effects spend

Minimum $300k qualified spend. Credits are fully transferable.

**How do I sell Louisiana credits?**
Work with a state-certified broker or entertainment lender. Credits are sold at 85–90 cents on the dollar. The state issues the credit certificate to the production company; the production then assigns it to the buyer. Settlement typically takes 4–8 weeks.

### New Mexico
**What is New Mexico's credit rate?**
New Mexico offers a **25–35% refundable credit**:
- 25% base on all qualified production expenditures (QPF)
- +5% rural uplift for productions shooting 60+ miles outside Santa Fe or Albuquerque city limits
- +5% TV series uplift for scripted series of 6+ consecutive episodes

No minimum spend threshold. Credits are refundable.

### Texas
**How does Texas work — credit or grant?**
Texas operates a **grant program** (not a tax credit), funded through the Texas Moving Image Industry Incentive Program. Grant rates:
- 15% base on qualified Texas expenditures
- +2.5% for underrepresented regions
- +2.5% workforce bonus (70%+ Texas-resident crew)
- +2.5% TV bonus (scripted series, 30+ min/episode)

Minimum spend: $250k for films, $100k for TV episodes. Grants are subject to annual program funding.

**What is the San Antonio local incentive?**
The City of San Antonio offers a **14% local production incentive** through Film San Antonio (filmsanantonio.com). This stacks with the Texas state grant for a verified combined maximum of **36.5%** on fully qualified spend (14% + 22.5% state). Productions shooting on City-owned properties also need a film permit from the San Antonio Film Commission.

### United Kingdom
**What does the UK offer?**
The UK provides **25% on qualifying UK production expenditure (QUKE)**. Productions must pass the BFI Cultural Test (minimum 18/35 points) and spend at least 10% of core expenditure in the UK. No minimum spend threshold. Administered by the British Film Institute.

## Using SceneIQ

### Dashboard
The Dashboard shows your active production portfolio with status indicators, budget totals, and recent activity. Use it as your starting point to track which productions need attention.

### Productions
Create and manage production records. Each production tracks title, type (film/TV/commercial), status (planning → pre-production → production → post-production → completed), and budget. Productions are linked to Calculator runs and AI Advisor sessions.

### Calculator
The Calculator computes the estimated incentive for a single production in a single jurisdiction. Select a production and a jurisdiction — the tool returns total expenses, qualified expenses, incentive amount, and effective rate based on current rules.

### Maximizer
The Maximizer compares incentives across multiple jurisdictions simultaneously. Select jurisdiction codes (e.g., TX + TX-AUSTIN), project type, and qualified spend — the tool returns a ranked comparison showing total incentive, effective rate, and the rules applied. Opt-in bonuses (local uplifts, content bonuses) are flagged as warnings rather than included in the base total, so you can evaluate them separately.

### Jurisdictions
Browse all supported jurisdictions, view their active incentive rules, and check the latest requirements extracted from official feed sources. Use this page to review what documentation, permits, and pre-certification steps each jurisdiction requires.

### Rule Review
The Rule Review panel surfaces pending rules extracted by the automated jurisdiction monitor. Rules are flagged as pending until a reviewer approves them. Approved rules flow into the Calculator and Maximizer engines.

### AI Advisor
The AI Advisor provides conversational guidance on incentive strategy. You can:
- Ask general questions about any jurisdiction or incentive type
- Select a **production context** in the left panel — the Advisor will generate production-specific suggested questions based on your project's budget, title, and status
- Use the **Production Questions** panel (appears when a production is selected) for tailored prompts like "What documentation should I be capturing on set?" or "Which jurisdictions qualify at this budget?"

The AI Advisor is a planning tool. Always confirm final figures with a qualified production accountant and the relevant state film office.

## Data and Compliance

### How current is the data?
Incentive rules are updated through two mechanisms:
1. **Automated feed monitoring** — the monitor runs against official film commission URLs and extracts new rules into the pending queue
2. **Manual seeding** — verified rules are inserted by SceneIQ staff after source verification

Rules have `effectiveDate` and `expirationDate` fields. The platform displays only active, currently-effective rules.

### What is the source of truth for each rule?
Each rule includes a `sourceUrl` and notes on where the data was verified. For final compliance decisions, always consult the official state film office website directly. Key sources:
- Georgia: georgia.org/film
- California: film.ca.gov
- New Mexico: nmfilm.com
- Louisiana: louisianaentertainment.gov
- Texas: gov.texas.gov/film
- San Antonio: filmsanantonio.com
- UK: bfi.org.uk/certification

### Are SceneIQ calculations legally binding?
No. SceneIQ provides estimates for production planning purposes. Incentive eligibility depends on detailed cost reports, CPA certification, and state office review. Always engage a qualified production accountant before committing to a location based on incentive projections.

## Technical

### Why does my login redirect to the wrong page?
Always use `http://localhost` (port 80 via nginx). The direct frontend port (`http://localhost:3000`) bypasses the API proxy and API calls will fail.

### How do I reset my session?
Log out from the top navigation or clear `pilotforge_token` from your browser's localStorage. The platform will redirect to the login page.

### I see stale data — how do I refresh?
Each page fetches data on mount. Navigate away and back, or refresh the page. The AI Advisor's conversation is session-only and clears on page reload.
